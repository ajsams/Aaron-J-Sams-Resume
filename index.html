My Career Journey

First Job: Professional Goat Feeder

I spent a lot of time at the zoo as a kid. This helped me develop a healthy respect for the natural world.

No Joke

Seriously. A lot of time at the zoo...

Music

I spent the first half of my college years at The University of Tennessee (Go Vols) studying vocal performance. This time taught me how to deal with professional feedback and use my nerves to my advantage when performing in front of an audience.

I also learned to paint on a mean jawline...

Culture

I studied abroad briefly in Classics, and it changed my life. A newfound fascination with- and appreciation for- human culture led me to pursue studies in Anthropology. This was my first major career pivot. Studying Anthropology taught me to both appreciate the value of data and science, but also to better understand that everything we do, including science, is filtered through- and influenced by-, our social and cultural circumstances.

I looked hard for Connor MacLeod here but couldn't find him.

Prehistory

Ultimately, I began my graduate studies in paleoanthropology, and in my second big pivot eventually completed a dissertation in anthropological genomics as a graduate student at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. Here, I taught myself to program (Python), developed a love of Resampling statistics, and learned how to tell a story with my work. I never had first access to data, so I also developed a knack for asking less than obvious questions of data.

This is my only photo with a celebrity.

Scaling Up

I continued to scale my work in evolutionary genomics as a Postdoctoral Researcher at Cornell University. Here, I scaled up my ability to work with large datasets and learned to work with high performance computing clusters. I grew to enjoy life in Ithaca so much that I decided to make a home here.

Embarking on a New Path

Cornell led me down a path the ended in another big pivot from studying humans to dogs when I joined a new startup, Embark Veterinary. I spent over seven years at Embark, doing research, building data pipelines, and leading teams to build useful products for dog owners, breeders, and veterinarians. At Embark I learned how businesses scale and grow, and how apply my scientific and technical skills to problems that are relevant to the business.

This was the first and I suspect only time my face will ever be on the Jumbotron and Madison Square Garden...

Back to Nature

I have always felt passionate about sustainability and acting to protect our planet. Heck, I went to a school called "Nature's Way" as a kid... I recently decided to take a career break, during which I've been refamiliarizing myself with the numerous challenges we face in tacking global warming, and the solutions available to us. I'm working on my next thing, likely to be another big pivot.

Stay tuned...
